Projects

  • Using convolutional neural networks for 3D species distribution modeling of Southern Ocean phytoplankton
    Abstract: The Southern Ocean is a large component of the global carbon cycle, and phytoplankton play a key role by converting CO2 to organic carbon, which can be transported to the deep ocean. Current studies examine phytoplankton presence and CO2 flux but do not take community species composition into account. In the past, we have explored training a classical machine learning model to predict species distributions. Now we aim to model phytoplankton community composition in the Southern Ocean using deep learning. [Poster]
